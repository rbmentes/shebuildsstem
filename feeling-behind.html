When You Feel Behind in Engineering...

February 2026

For a long time as an engineering student, I've carried a quiet feeling: the feeling of being behind.

Not just "a little behind"… but a feeling that follows you even after class ends — into your social life, your silent moments, and the way you see yourself.

Sometimes it feels like I need to work twice as hard to understand things others grasp immediately. I rewrite notes. I study for hours. I repeat the same topics again and again. And still… some things don't click.

💗 Retaking a Course Is Not Failure

One of the hardest parts of my journey was retaking some courses. It made me feel inadequate. As if I would never be good enough.

Movies show the "smart engineer" who understands everything instantly. But real life is not like that. Real engineering is messy, slow, and full of repetition — and that is completely okay.

⚡ Electrical Courses Were My Biggest Challenge

For me, electrical and circuit courses were the most difficult. Labs, problem-solving, building circuits… it wasn't easy. Honestly, it's still not fully easy.

Retaking those courses hurt my confidence deeply. I felt like I was failing.

🌷 But Something Changed

Even in my hardest courses, something shifted. I began enjoying certain parts. Working on electronics projects started to excite me.

When I had elective choices, I chose courses in these areas. Because despite the difficulty, there was still curiosity inside me — and I chose to follow it.

✨ Comparison Is a Trap

I compare myself to others. "They're ahead, I'm behind." But engineering is not about being perfect. It's about continuing — even when it's hard, even when it's slow, even when it hurts.

💗 Feeling Behind Is Not the End

If you're reading this and you feel the same way, I want you to remember:

  • 💗 You are not alone.
  • ⚡ You are not incapable.
  • 🌷 You are still growing.

"The engineers who changed the world weren't the ones who never struggled. They were the ones who kept going anyway."
