Part 2

CNNs for Speech Recognition

Beginner-friendly · Deep Learning · Speech Processing

In Part 1, we learned why raw audio is not directly suitable for ML models and why we use feature extraction methods like MFCC. Now we move to something more powerful: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs).

🧠 Why do we need neural networks for speech?

Traditional ML models rely on handcrafted features. But speech is complex — it contains patterns across time and frequency that are difficult to manually design features for. Instead of manually defining what is important, neural networks learn patterns directly from data.

📊 Turning audio into an image

CNNs were originally designed for image processing. The trick for speech is elegant — convert audio into a 2D representation and treat it like an image:

🎤
Raw audio
1D waveform over time
→
📊
Spectrogram
2D matrix: time × frequency
→
🖼️
Image input
CNN scans for patterns

A spectrogram shows how frequency content changes over time — it visually represents speech patterns that a CNN can then scan and detect.

🔍 How CNN works on speech

A CNN for speech processes the spectrogram image through a series of layers:

Conv

Convolution layers

Detect local patterns — frequency transitions, energy bursts, phoneme shapes

ReLU

Activation functions

Introduce non-linearity so the network can learn complex relationships

Pooling

Pooling layers

Reduce dimensionality — keep the most important features, discard detail

FC

Fully connected layers

Combine all learned features and output a final classification decision

What CNN filters learn from speech

🌊Frequency transitions
⚡Energy concentration
🗣️Phoneme structures
🌍Language patterns

⚙️ Why CNNs work well for speech

Capture local frequency-time patterns automatically
Handle noise better than traditional methods
Require far less manual feature engineering
Scale well with larger datasets

🧩 CNN vs MFCC — what's the difference?

🛠️

MFCC

Handcrafted feature extraction.

Human-designed rules decide what matters.

Fast, interpretable, limited.

🧠

CNN

Learning-based pattern extraction.

The model discovers what matters from data.

Slower to train, more powerful.
