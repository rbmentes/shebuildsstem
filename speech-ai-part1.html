When we record audio, what we actually get is a long sequence of numbers. These numbers represent air pressure changes over time.

What Is MFCC?

MFCC (Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients) is a compact representation of audio that mimics human hearing.

Instead of using raw waveform samples, we extract meaningful frequency-based features.

Why Is MFCC Important?

  • Reduces data size
  • Keeps important speech characteristics
  • Widely used before deep learning became dominant

Limitations

MFCC relies on handcrafted feature engineering and may miss complex patterns that deep learning models can learn automatically.
