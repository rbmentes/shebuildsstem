Understanding Audio Features & MFCC

What is sound to a machine — and how do we make it learnable?

When we record audio, what we actually capture is not "sound" in the way we feel it — we capture a long sequence of numbers representing air pressure changes over time. This is called a waveform. Speech, music, noise… everything becomes a signal.

🌊 Why can't we use raw audio directly?

You might think: "Why not just give the waveform to a machine learning model?" The problem is that raw audio is massive, noisy, and hard for classical models to interpret meaningfully.

Extremely long — thousands of samples per second
Very noisy with irrelevant detail
Hard for classical ML models to find patterns in

So instead of raw samples, we transform audio into something more meaningful: features.

✨ Feature extraction in speech AI

Feature extraction means converting audio into a compact representation that keeps the important information. For speech tasks, we want to capture:

🎵Pitch & tone
🗣️Pronunciation patterns
📊Frequency structure
🌍Language-specific cues

One of the most famous feature extraction methods is: MFCC.

💡 What is MFCC?

M
Mel A perceptual scale that matches how humans hear frequencies
F
Frequency The spectral content of the audio signal
C
Cepstral A mathematical transformation for compressing the spectrum
C
Coefficients The compact numerical output — usually 13 to 40 values per frame

MFCC represents speech by focusing on how humans actually hear sound — instead of every raw detail, it captures the shape of the spectrum in compact form.

⚙️ How MFCC is computed

1

Split audio into small frames

The audio signal is divided into short overlapping windows (typically 20–40ms each).

2

Apply Fourier Transform

Each frame is converted from time domain to frequency domain using FFT.

3

Map onto the Mel scale

Frequencies are mapped to the Mel scale — which mimics human hearing perception.

4

Take the logarithm

Log is applied to compress the dynamic range, matching how loudness is perceived.

5

Apply DCT to compress into coefficients

Discrete Cosine Transform compresses the result into a small set of coefficients — the final MFCC features.

⚠️ Limitations of MFCC

Handcrafted feature engineering — relies on human-designed transformations
May miss complex patterns that deep learning can discover automatically
Modern deep learning models consistently outperform MFCC-based approaches

🌸 My experience

In my Language Identification project, MFCC was the first method I tried. It helped me understand the basics of speech processing — and even though the results were limited, it gave me a strong foundation before moving to CNNs. You have to understand the classical approach to truly appreciate why deep learning works better.
